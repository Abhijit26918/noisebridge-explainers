Auxiliary Noise-Level Predictor

✕ Close
Purpose

The auxiliary head adds a multi-task regularisation signal by asking the model to predict the noise level of each noisy example. This forces the noisy representation \(\mathbf{z}^n\) to encode noise-level information explicitly, making it more structured and easier for the classifier to use.

Intuition: A representation that knows "this sentence is highly corrupted" can adjust its confidence accordingly. The auxiliary loss nudges the encoder to disentangle noise severity from semantic content — without the catastrophic failure of adversarial gradient reversal.

Equation
\[ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{aux}} = -\sum_{k=0}^{K-1} y_k^{\mathrm{noise}} \log\, q_\eta(\mathbf{z}^n)_k \]
Equation (5) — cross-entropy over K=4 noise levels {clean, low, medium, high}

Complete training objective

\[ \mathcal{L} = \underbrace{\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{CE}}}_{\text{classification}} + \alpha\,\underbrace{\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PWNIC}}}_{\text{contrastive}} + \gamma\,\underbrace{\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{aux}}}_{\text{noise pred.}} \]

α = 0.15 · γ = 0.2 · class weights 1.66:1 for L_CE

Symbol Reference
SymbolMeaning
q_ηAuxiliary classifier — a linear layer mapping z^n ∈ ℝ^768 → ℝ^4, shared weights across the batch
z^nNoisy sentence representation from the noise-aware gate (same z used for classification)
y^noise ∈ {0,1,2,3}One-hot noise level label: 0=clean, 1=low, 2=medium, 3=high
K = 4Number of noise levels in HindiMix-Noisy
α = 0.15PWNIC loss weight in the total objective
γ = 0.2Auxiliary loss weight — tuned so it regularises without dominating
Critical design: auto-disable

The auxiliary head is automatically disabled when fewer than 2 distinct noise levels appear in a training batch. This is essential — without it, the head receives a constant target (e.g. all "high") and the loss becomes trivially zero, which is harmless. But in the v1 design with GRL, a constant target caused catastrophic collapse.

❌ v1: GRL + adversarial head

Constant noise labels → adversarial predictor achieves zero loss instantly → GRL pushes encoder toward uniform representations → classification collapses.

XLM-R high noise: 0.777 (−5.2 F1 from baseline).

✓ v2: Aux head + auto-disable

No GRL. Constant noise labels → aux head auto-disabled → no gradient → no harm. When multiple noise levels present → regularises correctly.

XLM-R high noise: 0.835 (+0.56 over baseline).

Applied only to noisy stream

The auxiliary head is applied only to \(\mathbf{z}^n\) (the noisy representation), never to \(\mathbf{z}^c\). This is intentional — the clean representation should not be made noise-level-aware; it should remain as close to the original semantic space as possible.

During inference, only the clean stream classification head is used. The auxiliary head and PWNIC projection head are discarded after training — zero inference overhead.

Result
Ablation (XLM-R, seed 42)

Gate + PWNIC: 0.8401 → Gate + PWNIC + Aux: 0.8434 (+0.33). The auxiliary head provides a consistent further boost across all 3 seeds without any instability.

PWNIC — Phonetic-Weighted Noise-Invariant Contrastive Loss

✕ Close
Purpose

PWNIC aligns the representation of a clean utterance with its ASR-noisy version in a learned projection space. The key innovation: the alignment pressure is proportional to how phonetically different the two versions are. Pairs that are nearly identical contribute little; severely distorted pairs receive the strongest gradient.

Why this matters: Standard InfoNCE treats all positive pairs equally. But "hello" → "helo" (minor deletion) deserves far less correction force than "harami" → "hazrnis" (severe corruption). PWNIC encodes this distinction directly into the loss.

Step 1 — Phonetic Dissimilarity Weight
\[ \phi(x^c, \tilde{x}) = 1 - \frac{1}{M} \sum_{j=1}^{M} \mathrm{JW}(w_j^c,\, w_j^n) \]
Equation (3) — per-pair phonetic weight φ ∈ [0, 1]

Where \(M = \max(|x^c|_w, |\tilde{x}|_w)\) is the maximum word count, JW(·,·) is Jaro-Winkler string similarity, and unpaired words (from insertions/deletions) contribute similarity 0. So \(\phi = 0\) means the texts are identical; \(\phi = 1\) means completely disjoint.

Jaro-Winkler similarity — recap
\[ J(s_1,s_2) = \frac{1}{3}\!\left(\frac{m}{|s_1|} + \frac{m}{|s_2|} + \frac{m - t/2}{m}\right) \] \[ \mathrm{JW}(s_1,s_2) = J + \ell \cdot p \cdot (1-J) \]

\(m\) = matching characters within window; \(t\) = transpositions; \(\ell\) = common prefix length (≤4); \(p = 0.1\). Designed for short strings — perfect for comparing individual words.

Step 2 — PWNIC Objective
\[ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PWNIC}} = -\frac{1}{\displaystyle\sum_{i} \phi_i} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \phi_i \cdot \log \frac{ \exp\!\left(\mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{p}_i^c, \mathbf{p}_i^n) / \tau\right) }{ \displaystyle\sum_{\substack{k=1\\k\neq i}}^{2N} \exp\!\left(\mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{p}_i^c, \mathbf{p}_k) / \tau\right) } \]
Equation (4) — PWNIC loss · τ = 0.15 · α = 0.15 in total loss

Where \(\mathbf{p}_i^c = \pi_\xi(\mathbf{z}_i^c)\) and \(\mathbf{p}_i^n = \pi_\xi(\mathbf{z}_i^n)\) are projections through a 2-layer MLP \(\pi_\xi\). The denominator sums over all \(2N\) embeddings in the batch (N clean + N noisy) excluding the anchor.

Connection to InfoNCE

Standard InfoNCE (SimCSE)

All positive pairs weighted equally. Does not distinguish between trivially similar and severely distorted pairs.

PWNIC (ours)

Weight φ_i scales the contribution of each pair. When φ_i = 0 (no distortion), zero gradient. When φ_i is high (severe noise), maximum gradient pull.

Result
Ablation (XLM-R, seed 42)

Gate only: 0.8339 → Gate + PWNIC: 0.8401 (+0.62). Biggest single component gain. XLM-R benefits most because its SentencePiece tokenizer preserves phonetic structure that Jaro-Winkler can measure. mBERT/MuRIL WordPiece normalises this away.
